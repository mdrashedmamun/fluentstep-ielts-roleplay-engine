# Workplace Roleplay Variations

## Situation 1: Giving Feedback on a Proposal (Variation 1)
**Context:** Discussing a new project timeline with a colleague.
**Characters:** Taylor (lead), Alex (coordinator).

Taylor: Thanks for sending over the proposal, Alex.
Alex: No problem. What did you think?
Taylor: Overall, I see your ________. However, I have a slightly ________ view on the timeline.
Alex: Oh? Is it too ________?
Taylor: Possibly. From my ________, it might need some ________ to be realistic.
Alex: That’s fair. Maybe we could ________ the rollout and see?

## Answer Variations
1. point | Alts: reasoning, logic
2. different | Alts: alternative, broader
3. tight | Alts: critical, sensitive
4. perspective | Alts: point of view, experience
5. fine-tuning | Alts: refining, adjustment
6. pilot | Alts: phase, stagger

## Deep Dive & Usage
1. different view: A soft way to disagree without being negative.
2. point: Acknowledging their effort ("I see your point") before disagreeing.
3. fine-tuning: Implies the idea is good, but needs minor changes.
4. pilot: Very professional "corporate" English for "testing".

## Situation 2: Asking for Help (Variation 1)
**Context:** Needing assistance with a complex spreadsheet.
**Characters:** Sarah (busy), Sam (needing help).

Sam: Hey Sarah, do you ________ a minute?
Sarah: Yeah, what’s up?
Sam: Well, to be ________, I’m a bit ________ with this data.
Sarah: No worries. What do you need?
Sam: Could you do me a ________ and take a ________ look at this formula?
Sarah: Sure. Go ________.
Sam: Thanks, I really ________ your help.

## Answer Variations
1. have got | Alts: have, possess (unlikely)
2. honest | Alts: frank, upfront
3. swamped | Alts: overwhelmed, busy
4. favor | Alts: favor for me, hand
5. quick | Alts: brief, fast
6. ahead | Alts: on then, for it
7. appreciate | Alts: value, thank you for

## Deep Dive & Usage
1. have you got: Natural British/Common English for "do you have".
2. swamped: Great for admitting you're busy/overwhelmed professionally.
3. go ahead: Standard permission-giving.
4. appreciate your help: Professional gratitude.

## Situation 3: Handling a Mistake (Variation 1)
**Context:** A report was sent with the wrong numbers.
**Characters:** Manager (calm), Employee (responsible).

Employee: I’m sorry to ________ you, but something has ________ up.
Manager: What happened?
Employee: To be ________, I made a small mistake on the report.
Manager: I see. Well, let’s ________ down and look at it properly.
Employee: I’ve already ________ how to fix it. I’ll send a revised version ________.
Manager: Good. Lesson ________. Let’s make sure it doesn’t happen again.

## Answer Variations
1. interrupt | Alts: bother, disturb
2. come | Alts: cropped, turned
3. honest | Alts: frank, upfront
4. calm | Alts: sit, settle
5. figured out | Alts: worked out, ironed out
6. shortly | Alts: soon, in a bit
7. learned | Alts: noted, taken on board

## Deep Dive & Usage
1. something has come up: The perfect vague phrase for a problem.
2. calm down: Used here to suggest a cool-headed approach to a problem.
3. figured out: Collaborative and proactive phrasing.
4. lesson learned: Accepts responsibility and signals moving forward.
