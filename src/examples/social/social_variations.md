# Social Roleplay Variations

## Situation 1: Catching Up with an Old Friend (Variation 1)
**Context:** Two old friends meet at a park.
**Characters:** Sam (relaxed), Chris (busy but friendly).

Sam: Chris! Is that you? I haven’t seen you in ages.
Chris: Sam! What a surprise. How ________?
Sam: I’m ________, thanks. Just taking a stroll. What have you ________?
Chris: Not much, just ________ with work lately. By the way, did you hear about Sarah’s wedding?
Sam: No! That ________. When is it?
Chris: Next month. I still need to ________ if I can make it, though.

## Answer Variations
1. is it going | Alts: are you doing, have you been
2. doing well | Alts: not too bad, pretty good
3. been up to | Alts: been doing, been up to lately
4. swamped | Alts: busy, tied up
5. sounds like a plan | Alts: sounds good, sounds lovely (Note: "Sounds like a plan" used here to mean "That's good news")
6. make up my mind | Alts: decide, figure out

## Deep Dive & Usage
1. How is it going: The classic warm greeting for friends.
2. doing well: Natives favor "not too bad" for understatement.
3. swamped: A very natural way to describe being busy without sounding stressed.
4. make up my mind: Used for decisions that require some thought.

## Situation 2: Making Weekend Plans (Variation 1)
**Context:** Two colleagues deciding what to do on Saturday.
**Characters:** Jo (proactive), Alex (flexible).

Jo: Hey Alex, any plans for the weekend?
Alex: Not yet. I was thinking of just ________.
Jo: Why don’t we go for a hike? The weather is supposed to be ________.
Alex: That sounds ________. What time were you ________?
Jo: Maybe around ten?
Alex: Perfect. Let’s ________ on Friday to confirm the details.
Jo: Great. We can always ________ if the weather changes.

## Answer Variations
1. taking it easy | Alts: relaxing, staying in
2. lovely | Alts: nice, bright, pleasant
3. like a plan | Alts: good, great
4. thinking | Alts: planning, aiming
5. touch base | Alts: catch up, check in
6. play it by ear | Alts: see how it goes, decide then

## Deep Dive & Usage
1. taking it easy: High-value phrase for "relaxing".
2. lovely: British/IELTS 9 favorite for "good".
3. touch base: Professional but friendly way to say "let's talk later".
4. play it by ear: Idiom for being flexible.

## Situation 3: Meeting a New Neighbor (Variation 1)
**Context:** Welcoming someone who just moved in next door.
**Characters:** Mrs. Higgins (welcoming), Mark (new neighbor).

Mrs. Higgins: Hello there! I’m Mrs. Higgins from next door.
Mark: Oh, hi! I’m Mark. ________ you.
Mrs. Higgins: Welcome to the neighborhood. It’s quite ________ here, I hope you like it.
Mark: Thanks. It seems ________ so far.
Mrs. Higgins: If you ever need a ________, just let me know.
Mark: That’s very ________ of you. I appreciate it.

## Answer Variations
1. Nice to meet | Alts: Pleasure to meet, Good to meet
2. peaceful | Alts: quiet, residential, calm
3. lovely | Alts: nice, great, fine
4. hand | Alts: favor, bit of help
5. kind | Alts: thoughtful, nice

## Deep Dive & Usage
1. Nice to meet: Standard, can’t go wrong.
2. peaceful: Describes an area well—very IELTS friendly.
3. need a hand: Native idiom for "need help".
4. kind: Understated appreciation.
