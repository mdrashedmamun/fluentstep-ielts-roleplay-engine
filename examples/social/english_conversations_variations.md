## Situation 1: Shopping for Baking Supplies

**Context:** A customer is shopping at a grocery store and needs help finding baking ingredients.
**Characters:** Customer (nervous, first-time baker), Shop Assistant (helpful)

Customer: ________, ________? I'm looking for baking powder.
Shop Assistant: Sure! It's right here on this shelf. Are you baking something today?
Customer: Yes, I'm trying to make a cake, but I'm missing a few things. I also need flour.
Shop Assistant: Yes, you got it right—flour! It's on the shelf below the baking powder shelf. By the way, is this your first time baking?
Customer: Yes, and I'm so nervous. My friend told me to wing it, but I'm not sure what that means.
Shop Assistant: Oh, it means to try without following exact steps or recipes.

### Answer Variations
1. Excuse me | Alts: pardon me, sorry, I'm sorry
2. can you help me | Alts: could you help me, could you assist me
3. wing it | Alts: improvise, just try it, do it your way

### Deep Dive
- **"Excuse me"**: Can mean either asking for forgiveness ('I'm sorry') or getting attention. Context determines meaning.
- **"wing it"**: An idiom meaning to try something without preparation or a plan.

## Situation 2: Shopping Debate - Healthy vs. Fun

**Context:** Two shoppers deciding whether to buy healthy food or indulgent items.
**Characters:** Sarah (health-conscious), Max (fun-loving)

Sarah: Do we really need five boxes of cereal?
Max: ________! They're on sale!
Sarah: But we only eat breakfast once a day, right?
Max: True, but think of all the flavors!
Sarah: You mean all the sugar? Just ________ after that.
Max: Deal! Oh, what about chips?
Sarah: I thought we came here to buy healthy food only!
Max: You should have cheat days for yourself. It's when you eat less healthy food. For example: cheese, hamburger, pizza and so on.

### Answer Variations
1. Of course | Alts: absolutely, definitely, for sure
2. don't drink coffee | Alts: don't have caffeine, just relax
3. cheat day | Alts: treat day, indulgence day, special day

### Deep Dive
- **"cheat day"**: A day when you allow yourself to eat less healthy foods. Common in wellness culture.
- **"on sale"**: When items are discounted in price—very common shopping phrase.

## Situation 3: Ordering at a Smoothie Cafe

**Context:** Customers ordering drinks at a health-conscious cafe.
**Characters:** Barista (employee), Customer 2 (indecisive), Customer 3 (health-focused)

Barista: What can I get for you today? Do you have any fresh smoothies?
Customer 2: Yes! We have strawberry, mango, and even a green detox one with spinach and kale.
Customer 2: Hmm, spinach and kale? That sounds healthy, but maybe a little boring.
Barista: Well, if you're looking for something fun, we have a chocolate peanut butter smoothie.
Customer 2: Oh, that sounds like a cheat day in a cup!
Customer 3: I'll stick to something healthy. I'll have the green detox smoothie.

### Answer Variations
1. fresh smoothies | Alts: fresh juices, homemade smoothies
2. green detox | Alts: green smoothie, veggie smoothie
3. cheat day in a cup | Alts: guilty pleasure, indulgent treat

### Deep Dive
- **"stick to"**: Meaning to choose and stay with one option. "I'll stick with the healthy choice."
- **"detox"**: A wellness term meaning to remove toxins; commonly used for healthy drinks.

## Situation 4: New Year's Planning

**Context:** Friends discussing their goals and plans for the upcoming year.
**Characters:** Friend 1 (motivated), Laura (thoughtful)

Friend 1: Have you made any plans for the new year, Laura?
Laura: Not yet. I'm thinking about setting some personal goals. How about you?
Friend 1: I want to start a fitness routine. Maybe join a gym and get healthier.
Laura: That sounds great! I'm thinking of learning to cook. I hardly ever make home-cooked meals.
Friend 1: Cooking is a useful skill. Maybe you can take a cooking class.

### Answer Variations
1. new year plans | Alts: new year resolutions, new year goals
2. personal goals | Alts: self-improvement goals, life goals
3. fitness routine | Alts: exercise routine, workout plan
4. home-cooked meals | Alts: home-made food, cooked from scratch

### Deep Dive
- **"New Year's resolutions"**: Promises or plans people make at the start of a new year.
- **"personal goals"**: Individual objectives for self-improvement.

## Situation 5: Christmas Eve Preparations

**Context:** Friends talking about their Christmas Eve plans and celebrations.
**Characters:** Emily (family-oriented), Alex (baker)

Emily: Hi Alex! I've been helping my family decorate the Christmas tree. And you?
Alex: I've been baking cookies with my little sister. We made chocolate chip and gingerbread cookies.
Emily: That sounds delicious! Do you have any special plans for tonight?
Alex: Yes, we're having a family dinner and then we'll exchange gifts. What about you?

### Answer Variations
1. decorate the Christmas tree | Alts: put up the tree, trim the tree
2. chocolate chip cookies | Alts: chocolate cookies, chip cookies
3. gingerbread cookies | Alts: gingerbread, festive cookies
4. family dinner | Alts: holiday dinner, Christmas dinner
5. exchange gifts | Alts: open presents, give gifts

### Deep Dive
- **"gingerbread"**: A spiced cake/cookie traditional during Christmas.
- **"exchange gifts"**: To give each other presents; a common Christmas tradition.
- **"special plans"**: Unique or important arrangements for a special occasion.
